Poached Egg

To poach an egg, simmer 2–3 inches of water in a pot with 1 tbsp white vinegar, then create a gentle vortex with a spoon. Carefully drop a fresh, cold egg into the center and cook for 3-4 minutes, until the white is set but the yolk remains runny. Remove with a slotted spoon.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Water
  • Vinegar
  • Salt/Pepper

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the water: Bring a pot of water to a gentle boil, then turn the heat down to a low, barely-there simmer. Add 1 tablespoon of vinegar.
  2. Strain the egg (optional): Crack the egg into a fine-mesh sieve set over a bowl to remove the thin, watery white, then transfer to a small ramekin.
  3. Create a vortex: Stir the water in a circular motion with a spoon to create a whirlpool.
  4. Add the egg: Carefully tip the egg into the center of the vortex.
  5. Cook: Set a timer for 3 minutes for a runny yolk (3.5 - 4 minutes for firmer)
  6. Remove and drain: Lift the egg out with a slotted spoon and pat dry on a paper towel.
  7. Serve: Season with salt, pepper, or fresh herb
